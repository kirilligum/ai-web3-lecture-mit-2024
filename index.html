Bittensor: A Decentralized AI Network ($5B Market Cap)

  • Decentralized AI Marketplace
  • Participants earn TAO tokens for contributions
  • Integrates deep learning with blockchain consensus
  • Enables efficient and scalable AI computations
  • Notable user: Nous Research

DisTrO (Distributed Training Over-The-Internet)

  • Optimizer developed by Nous Research
  • Reduces inter-GPU communication by up to 10,000x
  • Enables decentralized AI development over standard internet connections
  • Allows training of large AI models without centralized data centers
  • Open-source collaboration fostering innovation
  • Successfully pre-trained a 15-billion parameter language model

Missing Components for an Ideal AI Web3

Peer-Reviewed Data

  • Necessity for high-quality data
  • A system similar to academic peer review is needed

Efficient GPU Rollup

  • On-chain AI computations are currently expensive
  • Solutions like Ora and Cartesi are working on optimization

Potential Advancements

  • AI Voting (DAO Delegates): AI filters and votes on behalf of users
  • Attestation: Compensate authors and creators for their data
  • Artists receive payment when their work is used to generate new content

Value of Modern AI

Abilities

  • Access to vast knowledge and analysis
  • Advanced planning and strategic thinking
  • Judging and evaluating complex scenarios
  • Proficient use of various tools
  • Creativity and innovation
  • Mixing and substituting concepts
  • Handling text and multimodal data

Impact

  • Replacing roles traditionally held by junior workers
  • Writing and auditing smart contracts
  • Summarizing vast amounts of information
  • Enhancing communication through chat interfaces
  • Generating diverse content
  • Explaining Web3 concepts and providing developer support

Resources

  • Data: Foundational element for training AI models
  • Training Compute: High computational power required
  • Inference: Resources needed for real-time model execution
  • Tools: Software and platforms facilitating AI development

Value of Blockchain

Decentralized Oversight

  • Blockchain as an ideal centralized overseer without a single point of control

Ledger Cloning

  • Duplicates the ledger for currency operations
  • Ensures security, transparency, and traceability

Privacy Technologies

  • Zero-Knowledge Machine Learning (ZKML): Allows proof of model execution without revealing data
  • Enhances AI privacy

Contract Cloning & Communal Ownership

  • Replaces traditional escrow services
  • Facilitates marketplaces and loan agreements
  • Enables tokenization of projects
  • Simplifies collective ownership and governance

Regulatory Advantages

  • Often faces lower regulatory hurdles
  • Offers scalable infrastructure similar to services like Stripe

AI x Web3 Integration

Shared Benefits

  • Transparency with optional privacy (FHE, ZKML)
  • Micro and international payments
  • Ownership and control through DAOs

Data

  • High-quality data acquisition through peer-review systems
  • Decentralized publishing ensures authenticity
  • Micropayments enable cost-effective operations
  • Attestation compensates creators and protects against deep fakes

Training

  • Combining computational resources globally
  • Trusted training environments established worldwide
  • Data traceability ensures models can be audited

Inference

  • Monetization through attestation and micropayments
  • Crowd-sourced knowledge enhances models
  • Technologies: Ora, Cartesi, oracles, TEEs

Tools

  • Oracles provide real-world data to blockchain applications
  • Verified Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)
  • Zero-Knowledge RAG integrates ZK-oracles for privacy

Prompts

  • Marketplace for selling prompts and AI agents
  • AI DAO delegates make informed decisions based on user preferences

Difficulties

Technological Limitations

  • GPU rollups not yet available
  • ASICs and photonics are emerging technologies
  • ZKML and FHE are currently expensive
  • Security concerns with TEEs
  • Trust issues with oracles

Data Quality and Expertise

  • Progress requires data created by experts
  • Low-cost labor is insufficient for high-quality data

Talent Acquisition

  • Attracting AI researchers is challenging
  • They prefer established teams over higher pay

Investment Hurdles

  • Some investors remain hesitant about AI and Web3
  • AI projects traditionally easier to fund than Web3
  • Web3 ventures may need incubators and focus on token listings
